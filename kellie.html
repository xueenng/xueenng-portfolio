the true story

A castle that was never finished

In 1915, a Scottish planter named William Kellie Smith began building a mansion unlike anything Malaya had seen - brick and marble shipped from India, a tower for looking over his rubber estate, plans for an indoor tennis court and the country's first elevator. Seventy craftsmen came from Madras to raise it.

Then the Spanish flu swept the estate and took many of the workers. A temple was built nearby to calm the spirits - if you visit it today, you will find a small figure of a white man in a white suit standing among its deities. It is Kellie Smith.

In 1926, on a trip to Lisbon to collect the elevator, he died of pneumonia. He was 56. His family left Malaya and never came back. The castle stopped exactly where it stood - corridors that end in open sky, stairways to floors that were never built, and, locals say, secret tunnels below and footsteps above that no one has ever traced.

the project

So I rebuilt it - and locked you inside

Echoes in Kellie's Castle is my final year project at UTAR: a VR escape room set inside the real castle. Heritage sites struggle to reach young visitors - my answer was to make the history something you solve, in the dark, with a candle in your hand.

I modelled the castle in Blender from historical research - the façade, the square tower, the staircases - then built the game in Unity (C#, OpenXR): three levels of story-driven puzzles. You piece together the diary of the castle, answer for its dates, reveal clues by candlelight, reassemble the floor plan against a timer, and crack the number that opens the final door: 1915.

  • 90–200 FPS against the 72 FPS VR comfort bar
  • 25 / 25 system test cases passed
  • 90% of testers gave the top rating for making them want to know Kellie's Castle
  • Runs on Meta Quest 2, desktop and Android
